# assurance-third-party-reporting

- Objective: Prepare evidence-backed assurance, third-party, maturity, and reporting artifacts without making unsupported claims.
- Trigger: User asks for audit evidence plans, control testing notes, issue closure packages, vendor risk summaries, questionnaire answers, maturity reporting, KPIs, KRIs, or executive summaries.
- Inputs: Control objectives, audit request, evidence set, remediation status, vendor scope, questionnaire, maturity model, metrics, source data, and audience.
- Primary owners: `assurance-evidence-remediation-agent` for assurance and remediation; `third-party-maturity-reporting-agent` for vendor, maturity, and reporting.
- Reviewers: `independent-assurance-reviewer` before external sharing, audit response, or closure.
- Steps: Confirm period and audience; classify evidence type; map claims to source evidence; draft responses; identify stale or missing evidence; prepare approval package.
- Validation gates: Claims have evidence, dates, owners, and limitations; metrics define source, calculation, cadence, and owner.
- Stop conditions: Request to submit externally, unsupported effectiveness claim, missing evidence, privileged repository access, contractual interpretation, or certification.
- Outputs: Evidence matrix, audit-response draft, vendor summary, questionnaire draft, maturity score rationale, metrics pack, and review findings.
- Human approvals: Required for audit submissions, vendor acceptance, customer-facing responses, executive reporting, and issue closure.
