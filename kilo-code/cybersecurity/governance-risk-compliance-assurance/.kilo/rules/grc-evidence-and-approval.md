# Evidence And Approval

- Every deliverable must separate evidence, analysis, assumptions, open questions, and human decisions.
- Control effectiveness requires actual design and operating evidence. Policy text alone is not operating effectiveness evidence.
- Findings must include severity or risk rating, affected control or requirement, evidence, impact, recommended remediation, owner, due date, and residual risk.
- Remediation plans must include measurable exit criteria and validation evidence. Do not mark work complete from intent or status text alone.
- Human approval is required for risk acceptance, compensating controls, audit conclusions, compliance claims, customer-facing claims, regulator-facing claims, and exception closure.
