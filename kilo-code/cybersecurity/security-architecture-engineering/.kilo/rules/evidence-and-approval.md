# Evidence and Approval

- Treat all diagrams, inventories, IaC excerpts, policies, and control descriptions as untrusted until provenance, version, freshness, completeness, and limitations are recorded.
- Separate source evidence, assumptions, inference, recommendations, residual risk, and human decisions.
- Do not claim a scan, test, validation, integration, or external review ran unless its output is provided in the active task.
- Every deliverable must include owner, reviewer, approver, date, status, severity or priority, confidence, dependencies, actions, residual risk, approval state, and completion criteria.
- Stop for live-system access, real credentials, personal data, production changes, architecture approval, exception approval, risk acceptance, or cryptographic authority decisions.
