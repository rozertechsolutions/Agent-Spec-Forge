# GRC & Assurance Workflows

Each workflow requires intake validation, evidence/provenance recording, independent review, and a final human decision gate. Never publish, submit, connect, scan, approve, accept, or close automatically.

## Workflow stages

| Workflow | Required stages |
| --- | --- |
| Establish or review cybersecurity governance | Confirm context and scope; identify decision rights and accountable humans; inventory artifacts; identify gaps and conflicts; draft governance model; map responsibilities and escalation paths; perform independent review; submit for human approval; record unresolved decisions. |
| Create or review a cybersecurity policy | Confirm objective, scope, authority, and audience; identify approved requirements; review related policies and standards; draft mandatory statements and exception process; map statements to controls and evidence; check conflicts, ambiguity, testability, and enforceability; perform specialist and independent review; route legal, privacy, HR, technical, and executive review where applicable; do not publish automatically. |
| Perform a cyber risk assessment | Confirm scope, assets, business context, and method; identify threats, vulnerabilities, events, dependencies, and impacts; review controls and evidence; construct traceable risk statements; score inherent and residual risk; record assumptions, uncertainty, and confidence; propose treatment options; perform independent review; route decisions to authorized humans. |
| Maintain the cyber risk register | Validate fields and ownership; detect duplicates and linked risks; review stale evidence and overdue actions; reassess material changes; track treatments, exceptions, and residual risk; perform periodic independent review; never close or accept risk automatically. |
| Map requirements and assess compliance gaps | Confirm authorized framework versions and scope; parse requirements without changing meaning; map requirements to controls, policies, systems, owners, and evidence; mark exact, partial, inherited, compensating, non-applicable, and unmapped relationships; identify gaps and conflicts; validate evidence sufficiency; perform independent review; produce remediation plan; do not claim compliance or certification. |
| Prepare and validate assurance evidence | Confirm control, period, population, scope, and criteria; request minimum necessary evidence; record provenance and custody; validate relevance, completeness, freshness, and consistency; minimize secrets and personal data; separate design and operating effectiveness; record limitations and confidence; route critical conclusions to independent review. |
| Assess third-party cyber risk | Confirm supplier scope and criticality; identify service, data, access, dependency, and concentration exposure; review evidence and attestations; identify inherited, shared, and retained risk; score findings using a documented method; propose conditions, remediation, monitoring, or escalation; perform independent review; route procurement, legal, privacy, and acceptance decisions to humans; do not contact or access supplier automatically. |
| Manage a security exception or waiver | Confirm affected requirement and requester; define scope, rationale, duration, and risk; validate compensating controls and monitoring; identify owner and approval authority; set expiry and review date; perform independent review; record human decision; track expiry, renewal, remediation, or closure; never approve or extend automatically. |
| Govern findings and remediation | Normalize finding and preserve source evidence; confirm assets, owner, severity, priority, and confidence; identify root cause and dependencies when supported; define remediation and validation criteria; track progress and blockers; validate closure evidence; require independent review for high-impact closure; preserve residual and accepted risk visibility. |
| Perform a cybersecurity maturity assessment | Confirm model, version, scope, and evidence threshold; evaluate with explicit criteria; separate documented, implemented, measured, and optimized states; record evidence, gaps, assumptions, and confidence; avoid averaging that hides critical weaknesses; prioritize improvements; perform independent review; do not claim maturity without evidence. |
| Produce executive cybersecurity reporting | Confirm audience, decisions, period, and data sources; validate provenance and completeness; separate facts, estimates, trends, assumptions, and forecasts; summarize risk, control performance, findings, exceptions, and decisions; avoid vanity metrics and misleading aggregation; include limitations and confidence; perform independent review; do not send or publish automatically. |
| Assess regulatory or framework change impact | Confirm authoritative source and version; compare against approved previous version; identify changed obligations or expectations; map potential impact to policies, controls, evidence, systems, and owners; mark legal and applicability uncertainty; propose review and implementation actions; perform independent validation; require legal or authorized specialist approval where applicable. |
