# Offensive Validation Workflows

Use these workflows as static planning guidance only.

1. Authorize and scope: require explicit written authorization, targets, exclusions, dates, objectives, permitted techniques, prohibited actions, test accounts by role, data handling, communications, deconfliction, emergency stop, third-party dependencies, production risk, safety dependencies, and stop on ambiguity.
2. Penetration-test plan: confirm scope, objectives, technology, environment, constraints, evidence needs, phases, hypotheses, methods, safe validation, rate limits, approved test data, stop conditions, cleanup, reporting, least-intrusive approach, and independent safety review.
3. Adversary-emulation or red-team campaign: confirm threat basis, objectives, authorization, white team, scope, duration, permitted behaviors, forbidden actions, phases, expected controls, observables, success metrics, deconfliction, safety, cleanup, and executive/legal/technical/safety approval as applicable.
4. Purple-team exercise: confirm behavior, systems, telemetry, detections, response objectives, participants, test cases, expected observables, evidence, pass criteria, improvement actions, white-team coordination, and incident-response handoff.
5. Social-engineering assessment: confirm legal, HR, privacy, executive, and target-owner approval; define population, exclusions, scenario, content, support, data minimization, stop conditions, reporting, ethics review, and no contact.
6. Finding documentation: confirm evidence, affected scope, preconditions, reproducibility, impact, confidence, minimum sensitive evidence, severity, business priority, remediation, safe retest criteria, and independent review for high impact.
7. Simulation governance: confirm approved use case, controls, content source, boundaries, schedule, approvals, observables, rollback, interpretation limits, disabled integrations, and no execution.
8. Cleanup and retest: inventory planned accounts, files, configurations, sessions, test data, and artifacts; define cleanup owner, evidence, validation, escalation, renewed retest authorization, exact scope, success criteria, variants, bypass review, status, and independent human closure.
9. Quality and safety review: verify authorization, scope, dates, methods, evidence, severity, cleanup, reviewer independence, scope creep, unsupported impact, unsafe techniques, missing artifacts, and incomplete cleanup.
