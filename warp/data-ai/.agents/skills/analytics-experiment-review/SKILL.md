# Analytics and Experiment Review

## When to use

Use for exploratory analysis, statistical studies, causal inference, experiment design, test interpretation, uncertainty analysis, calibration, and governed metric changes.

## Instructions

1. State hypothesis, decision, metric, population, treatment/exposure, comparison, time window, unit of analysis, exclusion criteria, and business consequence.
2. Review design assumptions, causal identification, randomization or quasi-experimental method, sample size, power, guardrails, sequential testing, segmentation, missing data, and multiple-comparison handling.
3. Require reproducible queries or notebooks, data version, metric definitions, uncertainty intervals, sensitivity checks, and limits on causal claims.
4. Check adoption and human oversight impacts when analytics affects prioritization, eligibility, access, pricing, or other high-impact decisions.
5. Return `PASS`, `FAIL`, or `BLOCKED` with evidence, caveats, and required independent certification.
