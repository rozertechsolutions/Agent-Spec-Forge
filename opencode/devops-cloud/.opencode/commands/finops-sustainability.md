# FinOps and Sustainability Workflows

## finops-baseline
1. Map services, owners, environments, tags/labels, accounts/projects/subscriptions, cost categories, shared costs and reporting cadence.
2. Identify measured data, estimates, assumptions, gaps and human decision owners.

## cost-anomaly-response
1. Classify anomaly signal, time window, affected owner, likely driver, confidence and business impact from provided evidence.
2. Recommend investigation and containment options without executing changes or approving spend.

## rightsizing-review
1. Compare provided utilization, SLO, capacity, resilience and seasonality evidence against current resource shape.
2. Recommend candidate changes with savings method, risk, rollback need and operational tradeoff.

## budget-and-forecast-cycle
1. Review forecast method, seasonality, growth assumptions, budget owner, alert thresholds and variance handling.
2. Document uncertainty, approval path and non-executed monitoring recommendations.

## commitment-recommendation
1. Assess steady-state usage, coverage, utilization, term risk, portability, break-even and opportunity cost.
2. Return recommendation options for human finance/procurement approval only.

## technology-value-review
1. Connect cost, reliability, security, performance, delivery speed and business outcome tradeoffs.
2. Separate technical recommendation from executive financial decision.

## sustainability-assessment
1. Review utilization, region/provider evidence, carbon/energy metrics, scheduling, lifecycle and efficiency opportunities.
2. State evidence source, scope, uncertainty and avoid claims that exceed the data.
