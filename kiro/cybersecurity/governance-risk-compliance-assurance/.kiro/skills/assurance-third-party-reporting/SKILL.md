# assurance-third-party-reporting

- Objective: Prepare assurance and reporting outputs that make evidence status, gaps, and ownership visible.
- Trigger: User asks for control testing support, evidence request lists, audit readiness, third-party risk review, maturity metrics, executive reporting, or remediation status.
- Inputs: Control scope, evidence inventory, vendor data, issue register, audit requests, maturity criteria, reporting period, audience, and source artifacts.
- Primary owners: `assurance-evidence-remediation-agent` for assurance and evidence; `third-party-maturity-reporting-agent` for vendor, maturity, and reporting work.
- Reviewers: `independent-assurance-reviewer` for final review.
- Steps: Confirm audience and reporting period; map controls or vendors to evidence; classify evidence as available, missing, stale, or inferred; identify gaps and owners; prepare concise status and remediation asks.
- Validation gates: Every assertion links to evidence or an explicit assumption; every gap has owner, due date, risk, and escalation status when available.
- Stop conditions: User asks for audit sign-off, vendor approval, compliance certification, unsupported maturity claim, or external distribution.
- Outputs: Evidence request list, assurance status, vendor risk summary, maturity score rationale, remediation dashboard narrative, and reviewer findings.
- Human approvals: Required for audit readiness claims, vendor decisions, maturity scores used externally, and customer- or regulator-facing reports.
