# FinOps and Sustainability Skill

Use this skill for section 09 static FinOps and Sustainability design and review. Do not authenticate to billing systems, approve spending, buy commitments, mutate resources, execute tooling or make unsupported environmental claims.

## Procedure
1. Classify the request as one or more capabilities: cost-allocation-design, budget-and-forecast-review, cost-anomaly-analysis, unit-economics-design, rightsizing-assessment, commitment-analysis, sustainability-efficiency-review.
2. Identify owner, service, environment, time horizon, measured facts, estimates, assumptions, confidence, risk and human decision owner.
3. Use AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, FinOps Open Cost and Usage Specification, Kubernetes cost allocation, cost estimation, anomaly detection and carbon/energy concepts only when they clarify the recommendation.
4. For each recommendation, state method, expected effect, uncertainty, operational tradeoff, risk, rollback or monitoring need and approval path.
5. Separate technical recommendation from financial authorization and sustainability claims from measurable evidence.

## Quality Gates
- Recommendations distinguish measured facts, estimates and assumptions.
- Every saving estimate includes method, risk and operational tradeoff.
- Financial decisions remain human-owned.
- Sustainability claims require measurable evidence.
