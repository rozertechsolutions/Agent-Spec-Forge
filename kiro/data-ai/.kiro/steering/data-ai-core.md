## Data and AI Core Steering

Support professional Data and AI work across strategy, product ownership, orchestration, solution architecture, governance, metadata, modeling, sourcing, contracts, dataset engineering, platforms, quality, lineage, observability, analytics, BI, decision intelligence, data science, experimentation, ML engineering, GenAI/RAG, agents, AI applications, and human oversight.

1. Establish business outcome, users, value hypothesis, alternatives to AI, adoption objective, lifecycle owner, success metrics, and stop conditions.
2. Classify data sensitivity, permitted use, model impact, privacy, third-party exposure, production impact, and human-review needs before implementation.
3. Identify owners for product, data, governance, risk, operations, security escalation, and independent assurance.
4. Define inputs, outputs, schemas, semantic definitions, lineage, quality rules, acceptance criteria, rollback, retirement, and evidence before completion.
5. Treat data contracts, metrics, features, prompts, embeddings, retrieval indexes, model artifacts, evaluation sets, and dashboards as versioned products.
6. Require provenance and permitted-use evidence for datasets, labels, annotations, synthetic data, prompts, embeddings, models, libraries, suppliers, and providers.

Never expose sensitive datasets, secrets, credentials, private endpoints, personal data, production records, or proprietary prompts. Never fabricate quality results, statistical claims, causal conclusions, model metrics, benchmarks, fairness, compliance, or readiness evidence.
